Question: Why is it important to clarify your brand strategy and brand voice before using AI tools like ChatGPT for content?
Answer: If you don’t define who your brand is, AI tools will produce generic, personality-free content that sounds like everyone else. When you don’t clarify your brand’s personality and values, the market fills in the gaps for you, diluting your uniqueness. Defining a brand voice makes your business stand out—it gives your communications that sense of "a brand is like a person."
Key Insight: Brand clarity is fundamental; it ensures your communication is distinct and memorable, preventing your business from blending in with the crowd using AI-generated content.

Question: What first steps should someone take to start defining their brand voice using ChatGPT?
Answer: Begin by accessing the Brand Voice Builder in ChatGPT (need a paid plan for custom GPTs). The process starts with simple prompts: your name and business name, then moves to questions about your brand’s personality (e.g., “If your brand was a person at a party, how would it show up?”), values, target demographic, and other specifics. Answer each step honestly and thoughtfully. 
Key Insight: Systematic, guided questions help extract the aspects that make your brand unique and set the foundation for a powerful brand strategy.

Question: What kinds of values and guiding principles should be articulated for your brand?
Answer: Your brand’s values are its non-negotiable principles, such as authenticity, trust, listening to clients, being results-driven, and not being swayed by money over integrity. You should define these in ways unique to you (e.g., “Life is better with friends” instead of just “community”). These values can become visible elements—like wall art—to reinforce your principles to your team and clients.
Key Insight: Specific, individualized articulation of principles gives your brand a soul and lasting differentiation.

Question: How should a brand determine and document its ideal customer feelings and experience?
Answer: Ask yourself how you want your clients to feel when they interact with your brand (e.g., comfortable, heard, valued, empowered, the priority). These become emotional goals and are integral to both service and marketing, ensuring communication and experience stay consistent.
Key Insight: Defining desired client feelings drives all interactions and content toward a focused, emotionally resonant outcome.

Question: What are brand archetypes, and how can they help define your brand?
Answer: Archetypes are classic, recognizable brand personalities (like Caregiver, Sage, Hero, Magician, etc.). Picking one or a blend that matches your brand helps solidify your brand’s tone and behavior. For example, a business described as “fun and wise” serving women 35–70 may lean toward the Caregiver and Sage archetypes.
Key Insight: The right archetype roots your brand’s communication and establishes a consistent, trusted identity in the minds of customers.

Question: What should a brand consider for its tone of voice and specific communication style?
Answer: Define the vibe you want—smart, warm, trustworthy, nurturing, playful, but not over-the-top. For some, tone might mean short and punchy posts, minimal storytelling, and no emojis due to professional or regulatory reasons. Exclude what doesn’t fit: salesy, faddish, overly corporate, or content that mentions restricted treatments.
Key Insight: Clarity around both what your voice is and is not helps maintain strong, on-brand content and protects against AI-driven blandness.

Question: How can a brand crystallize its essence into a tagline or summary?
Answer: Draft a tagline or summary sentence that directly expresses your brand’s promise and feel (e.g., “We’re here to empower you with honest skincare so you feel heard, cared for, and have the knowledge to make the best decisions for yourself.”). Avoid negative framing and focus on your client empowerment.
Key Insight: Well-crafted taglines rooted in your real values and client outcomes can clearly broadcast what sets you apart.

Question: How can you implement your completed brand strategy into a custom GPT for ongoing, consistent content?
Answer: Once your brand voice summary is complete, copy it and, within ChatGPT (on a paid Plus account via desktop), go to “Explore GPTs,” hit “Create,” paste the summary as instructions, give it a name and a role (e.g., “You are my copywriter”), set access, and save. Share the link with your team for unified content creation.
Key Insight: Embedding your customized brand knowledge into a GPT empowers your business to produce on-brand content quickly and keeps your team aligned.

Question: Why is it important to avoid certain elements (like emojis, em dashes, or medical language) in some brands’ communications?
Answer: For some industries, regulations or professionalism dictate communication style. For instance, a cosmetic clinic may avoid emojis and em dashes for a more professional tone and to sidestep rules about medical advertising. It’s also important to steer clear of salesy or unapproved medical terminology.
Key Insight: Consistent adherence to industry-specific communication standards maintains professionalism, builds trust, and keeps your brand compliant.

Question: What makes authentic and vulnerable content effective for brand engagement?
Answer: Real, open, and vulnerable posts (like talking about self-care after a hard year) tend to have high engagement because they demonstrate authenticity—people connect on a human level, rather than responding to only polished or perfect content.
Key Insight: Vulnerability and authenticity in communication can significantly increase engagement and set your brand apart from less genuine competitors.

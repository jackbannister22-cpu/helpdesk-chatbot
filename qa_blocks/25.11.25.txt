Question: How should I approach creating a tagline for my relationship education and training business, especially as I branch into courses?
Answer: The tagline for your business should communicate a few key messages: first, that great relationships are a learnable skill; second, that the business is warm and hopeful; and third, for your courses, you can use more outcome-driven phrases (like “Turn conflict into deeper connection”). For the overall umbrella tagline, choose something broad, with specific taglines for each course as needed. Collaborate with your team, ask for feedback, and use tools like ChatGPT to generate more variations. Most importantly, the tagline should feel intuitive and resonate with you and your ideal client.
Key Insight: The best taglines balance clarity (relationships are a skill), warmth, and inspiration. Test variations and ensure it feels natural to you and your audience.

Question: As someone who dislikes being on camera or social media, how can I create an effective video for my business website and social platforms?
Answer: If you’re uncomfortable appearing on camera or social media, consider recording a voiceover instead, overlaying it with visuals of your work (like framing, glass, or mirrors). You can write or amend your own script, then use AI (such as ChatGPT) to make it more emotive and “sell the dream” to the customer. The script should focus on the emotional impact your work creates for clients—less about you, more about their feelings and outcomes. A more emotive, customer-focused approach feels warmer and draws people in. AI can even clone your voice for future use with just one recording session.
Key Insight: You don’t need to appear onscreen to create compelling video content. Use your own voice to connect emotionally, and let visuals of your work do the rest.

Question: When my business is a finalist for an award, what are the best steps to maximize publicity if we win?
Answer: Write your press release before the results are announced, so you can quickly send it out if you win. Use ChatGPT to help draft it, making sure to frame the story as a “local business wins award,” since local media favor these angles. Make a spreadsheet of all local media contacts (newspapers, radio, magazines). Include quotes that share your journey and what winning means, ideally highlighting community impact. Prepare photos to attach, and check your press release structure in resources like Cleverbunch Module 9. When emailing, use a strong subject line (e.g., “Local business wins prestigious award”), reference the journalist’s work, attach your press release, and offer your contact details. Send on Monday, and then call key contacts (mid-morning, not afternoon) to ensure they received it. 
Key Insight: Preparation is key—have your announcement assets ready to enable timely, targeted outreach to local media, maximizing your visibility.

Question: Is it ethical and effective to reach out personally to people who regularly open and engage with my business newsletter, and how can I do this well?
Answer: Yes—if they’ve opted in to your list, it’s ethical to reach out, but avoid saying you’ve observed their behavior directly (like, “I see you open my emails”). Instead, personalize your messages by referencing their company and context. Have a clear, “soft” reason for reaching out—such as inviting them for a Zoom coffee to discuss their needs, making it about them (their pain points, goals), rather than yourself. Keep it short, polite, and offer a specific ask, like a 30-minute chat with a Calendly link. Use ChatGPT if you want help refining the message. 
Key Insight: Personalized outreach, framed around genuinely learning about your subscribers’ needs, is both ethical and likely to yield valuable insights and deeper relationships.

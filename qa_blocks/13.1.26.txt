Question: How do you get the most value out of an Advisory Board session at Basic Bananas?
Answer: Share your screen and welcome all thoughts, experiences, and opinions, even if they are second-hand or represent what a customer might think. There’s no right or wrong; more brains make for better answers, and there’s no judgment. Anyone can throw their ideas into the ring at any time, and sessions are about everyone helping each other.
Key Insight: Open participation and a non-judgmental, collaborative environment drive advisory board success—bring ideas freely and benefit from collective wisdom.

Question: How should you approach structuring and launching a product suite after a change in business launch timing?
Answer: If your business launch timeline changes suddenly (e.g., due to a non-compete expiring earlier than expected), move quickly but build a structure by clarifying your product suite from the “freebie” entry offer to your highest-level package. Create a clear launch timeline, work backward from your deadline, and clarify each offering’s format. Advisory Board sessions can help refine this process step by step, and you can submit anything—from timelines to draft products—for feedback.
Key Insight: When timelines accelerate, structure your products and timeline first, then leverage advisory sessions for focused feedback, refining as you go instead of aiming for initial perfection.

Question: What’s the best way to define your product range, especially when serving women in business with financial/mindset programs?
Answer: Start by brain-dumping every format and delivery vehicle you could use (e.g., PDFs, live sessions, community groups, tailored surveys). Then outline your “main dishes” (core programs) and “with rice” (must-have basics everyone needs), so your offer becomes tangible and easy to understand. Map out a customer journey from first contact to high-level offerings, making it sequential if possible. This helps clarify which products to launch first and presents your services as a clear, stepwise transformation.
Key Insight: Productize service offerings with clear, sequential customer journeys and tangible options—people buy more easily when they see and understand “the menu.”

Question: Why should you avoid self-paced online courses if you’re worried about completion rates impacting your brand?
Answer: If participants in a self-paced course often don’t complete it, it can create a negative perception of the value or quality of your business, even if the real issue is with participant commitment. Consider interactive, group-based, or guided formats to maximize results and brand reputation.
Key Insight: Protect your reputation by delivering services in formats that support customer completion and satisfaction, not just ease of delivery.

Question: What’s the process for developing and refining your initial product and marketing assets in a new business?
Answer: Begin by getting rough versions of your product structure and marketing materials out of your head and onto paper—don’t wait for perfection. Bring drafts, scribbles, and outlines to advisory sessions for feedback, correction, and validation. Only move into paid design or development once the core concepts and messaging are road-tested with the group.
Key Insight: Early-stage product and brand development benefits from collaborative feedback—prioritize progress over perfection and get input early to save time and money.

Question: What’s the most important feature of a website homepage for a service business, and how should a headline be crafted?
Answer: The main headline (“hero text”) is the most important feature because it’s the number one most-read text and tells visitors what the business actually does. Make it large, clear, specific, and focused on the service or transformation you offer. Avoid vague or generic calls-to-action as your main headline. Ensure the supporting text under it is also strong and clear.
Key Insight: A homepage headline should instantly convey the core offer—it’s your business’s one chance at instant clarity and connection.

Question: How can visual design choices on a website impact user understanding and credibility?
Answer: Busy or moving backgrounds can reduce readability and make messages hard to see. Stock images feel generic; using real team photos and members adds authenticity. Use a logo that blends into the design, and move testimonials up to the homepage for immediate trust.
Key Insight: Prioritize clarity and human connection with simple, authentic visuals and layouts that spotlight your offer and team.

Question: What are best practices for communicating business values on a website?
Answer: List only 3–5 core values for clarity and retention. Too many keywords or buzzwords overwhelm visitors and blur your message. Make your language human and flowing, not like keyword stuffing.
Key Insight: Focus and simplicity in communicating values help visitors remember what your brand stands for.

Question: How can you improve customer journey and conversion for a local service business website?
Answer: Localize your headline (include the city or area served), showcase real photos of your local team or premises, create sector-specific FAQs to support relevant searches and conversions, and add a dedicated Reviews page. Display a company timeline that carries through to the present for a sense of growth and reliability.
Key Insight: Localization, authenticity, and tailored content drive trust and action from local customers seeking service providers.

Question: What are smart ways to optimize your LinkedIn profile as a business owner?
Answer: Use your profile banner to match your website and reinforce your headline. Include business-relevant keywords (e.g., your trade, location, and company name) in your title and About section to improve search visibility. Feature calls-to-action and regularly post content from your website. Request recommendations to build credibility, and encourage your team to mirror these elements for a unified brand presence.
Key Insight: Consistent, keyword-focused, and branded LinkedIn profiles extend your business’s reach and trust factor across digital channels.

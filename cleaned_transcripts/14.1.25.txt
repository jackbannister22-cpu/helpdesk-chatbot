Christo or Franziska: Sarah, let’s look at your Meta Ads strategy. You and your team are planning to test with a $10–15 per day budget at first, then increase. You’ve used ChatGPT to create the plan, but you want to make sure nothing's missing or overly rigid.

Christo or Franziska: ChatGPT did a solid job, but let's simplify for you and everyone. When you haven’t run paid ads before, look at marketing as a funnel. At the top is awareness—people who don’t know you yet. As the funnel narrows, people drop out, and a few make it to the bottom and buy.

Start with a top-of-funnel awareness campaign. This is informational and inspiring, not “buy now.” Use campaign ideas like “Have you ever dreamed of this experience?”—aspiration, inspiration, beauty. For awareness, use a small daily spend, and let Meta’s AI do most of the work—use automatic settings, placements, targeting. Their machine learning will optimise for you.

After a few weeks of awareness ads, once you see website visitors and engagement, you’ll have built a remarketing audience. Then, set up a retargeting campaign to those people—people who visited your site or watched your videos. Here, ask them to take action, like downloading a guide (“Top 10 Destinations” or your existing planning resource). Test two ads: (1) low-risk download (“discover secret locations…”), (2) higher-commitment (“get a quote”). Let both run, measure which converts.

In summary: phase one is awareness campaign, then phase two is retargeting to your engaged audience, offering a download or a call to action. Keep it measurable, track what you spend and the results. You can use ChatGPT for headline and copy ideas. It’s not two separate funnels, but a single funnel with multiple campaigns along the journey.

Christo or Franziska: It’s one funnel, just two different campaigns—awareness, then retargeting. Not two separate funnels.
